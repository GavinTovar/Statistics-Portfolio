# Statistical Computing & Research Portfolio

This repository is a curated collection of coursework, applied projects, and research work completed during my Master’s and PhD studies in Statistics at Oregon State University. It emphasizes computational statistics, simulation-based inference, and modern statistical modeling techniques.

## Overview

The materials in this repository reflect graduate-level training in statistical theory, methodology, and computing. The focus is on implementing, testing, and understanding statistical methods through simulation studies, applied data analysis, and research-oriented projects.

Core areas include:

- Statistical modeling and inference  
- Model selection
  - Sequential variable selection in survival analysis
  - Effects of imputation and resampling techniques in missing data
  - Sequential and regularization model selection
- Simulation-based methodology
- Generalized linear mixed models (GLMMs)
- Resampling methods
- Missing Data Explorations
  - EC and ECM implementations
  - Stan Usage

## Contents

### Coursework
Assignments and projects from graduate-level courses in statistics and computer science, covering topics such as:
- Mathematical statistics  
- Linear and generalized linear models  
- Data analysis and visualization  
- Algorithms and computing  

### Simulation Studies
Code and analyses exploring statistical behavior through simulation, including:
- Model selection effects on inference  
- Bias and variance under different estimation strategies  
- Missing data mechanisms (MCAR, MAR, MNAR)  
- Regularization and shrinkage methods  

### Research Projects
More in-depth work connected to ongoing research interests, including:
- Model selection and inference under uncertainty  
- Effects of human judgment in statistical decision-making  
- Methodological comparisons in survival and regression settings
- Missing data mechanisms 

### Presentations, Guides, Reports
Supporting materials for conference presentations, guest lectures, guides, and written research work.

## Tools & Technologies

- **Languages:** R, Python, C/C++, Stan, JavaScript
- **Statistical Computing:** Tidyverse, data.table, NumPy, SciPy  
- **Modeling:** GLMs, GLMMs, survival models, time series methods  
- **Simulation & Inference:** Monte Carlo methods, bootstrap, resampling techniques  
- **Development Tools:** Git, RStudio, Quarto, LaTeX  

## Research Focus

My work broadly focuses on understanding how statistical decisions affect inference, particularly in the context of:

- Model selection and selection-induced bias  
- Stability and interpretability of statistical procedures  
- Simulation-based evaluation of statistical methods  
- Practical implications of methodological choices in applied statistics
